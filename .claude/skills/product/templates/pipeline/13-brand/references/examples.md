# Step 5 — Brand Examples

Voice samples (good vs bad) and a ≥20-pair "we are / we are not" bank organised by product category. Use this as a writing template, not a copy-paste source — the founder voice is unique by definition.

---

## Voice Samples — Good vs Bad

The discipline: each sample is the *actual copy* the brand would emit on a *real* surface. If you can't picture the surface, don't write the sample.

### Example 1 — Error message

**Good** (developer tool, sardonic-direct voice):
```
That command doesn't exist. Did you mean `commit` or `import`? — type `?` for the full list.
```
Specific (names two near-misses), useful (gives next action), in-voice (the trailing em-dash + lowercase is the brand's tone).

**Bad** (same surface, brand-strategy filler):
```
We're sorry, but it looks like that command isn't recognized. Please check your input
and try again. We're here to help if you need assistance.
```
Apologetic-corporate, no actionable next step, voice could be any product on the planet.

### Example 2 — Onboarding welcome

**Good** (B2B SaaS, considered-warm voice):
```
Two minutes. We import your Jira issues, you decide what to keep. The free tier covers
your first 5 teammates — no credit card, no demo, no clock starting.
```
Sets expectation (2 minutes), names the friction (Jira import), surfaces the tier (no credit card / no demo) — the contrast `no demo` is the brand's stance.

**Bad** (same surface, generic-empowerment voice):
```
Welcome to your new productivity journey! We're excited to help you achieve more with
your team. Let's get started — your potential awaits.
```
Generic, content-free, indistinguishable from 200 other onboarding flows.

### Example 3 — Marketing tagline

**Good** (consumer app, restraint-as-luxury voice):
```
An issue tracker that takes up less of your day.
```
Inverts the category convention ("more features, more dashboards"); sharp, single-promise.

**Bad** (same product, hype-driven voice):
```
The next-generation, AI-powered platform that revolutionizes how teams collaborate.
```
Buzzword soup; "revolutionizes" + "next-generation" + "AI-powered" + "platform" is a slop accumulator.

### Example 4 — Support reply

**Good** (terminal-aesthetic dev tool, sardonic-direct voice):
```
Yep — that's a bug. Reproduced it locally, fixed in #4271. Ships in tomorrow's build;
your data is safe in the meantime. Sorry about that.
```
Owns the issue, names the fix, gives the timeline, doesn't over-apologize.

**Bad** (same surface, corporate-deflection voice):
```
Thank you for reaching out! We've received your concern and our team is investigating.
We appreciate your patience as we work to resolve this matter.
```
Information-free; doesn't name what's broken, who's looking at it, or when it'll be fixed.

### Example 5 — Paywall copy

**Good** (free-tier-graduating product, honest-restrained voice):
```
Free covers 5 active teammates. You're at 6. Upgrade to Pro ($8/seat/mo) or remove a
teammate from the active list — both work, both keep your data.
```
States the limit hit, gives two options (upgrade or stay), one of which is *not* "give us money".

**Bad** (same surface, scarcity-pressure voice):
```
You've hit your limit! Upgrade now to unlock the full power of our platform and never
worry about restrictions again. Limited-time offer — 20% off your first 3 months!
```
Manufactured urgency, fake scarcity, brand voice is "any other SaaS".

## We Are / We Are Not — Pair Bank

≥ 20 example pairs across product categories. The discipline: `**We are** X. **We are not** <near-adjacent-Y>.` — the contrast between X and Y must be a real distinction someone could mistake. "We are honest. We are not lying." is a tautology, not a discipline.

### Developer tools / dev infrastructure

1. **We are** direct. **We are not** blunt.
2. **We are** opinionated. **We are not** dogmatic.
3. **We are** terminal-grade. **We are not** ascetic.
4. **We are** keyboard-first. **We are not** keyboard-only.
5. **We are** specific about errors. **We are not** smug about correctness.

### B2B SaaS (mid-market, EM/PM tooling)

6. **We are** considered. **We are not** slow.
7. **We are** opinionated about defaults. **We are not** rigid about workflows.
8. **We are** transparent about pricing. **We are not** transactional in tone.
9. **We are** restrained. **We are not** sparse.
10. **We are** sardonic about the category. **We are not** cynical about the user.

### Consumer apps / lifestyle

11. **We are** warm. **We are not** sentimental.
12. **We are** playful. **We are not** childish.
13. **We are** confident. **We are not** smug.
14. **We are** honest about limitations. **We are not** apologetic about them.
15. **We are** quietly delightful. **We are not** maximalist about delight.

### Editorial / content / media

16. **We are** considered. **We are not** academic.
17. **We are** opinionated. **We are not** strident.
18. **We are** literary. **We are not** ornate.
19. **We are** human. **We are not** confessional.
20. **We are** willing to argue. **We are not** willing to bait.

### Financial / regulated

21. **We are** precise. **We are not** dry.
22. **We are** trustworthy. **We are not** stuffy.
23. **We are** clear about regulation. **We are not** legalese.
24. **We are** fluent in the domain. **We are not** showing off.

### Cross-category — the universal four

These four pairs work for almost any product. Use as a starter when the founder is stuck.

25. **We are** specific. **We are not** vague.
26. **We are** confident. **We are not** boastful.
27. **We are** honest about trade-offs. **We are not** falsely humble.
28. **We are** in the user's voice. **We are not** in the brand's marketing voice.

## How to use this bank

1. **Start with the always-ask anchors** in `prompt-bank.md` to surface 1–2 founder pairs.
2. **Match what you heard against this bank** — find pairs that overlap with the founder's articulation, then propose them back to the founder for confirmation/rejection. Fewer-but-confirmed beats more-but-imposed.
3. **Always include at least one rejection.** "**We are not** X" without a rejected alternative the brand *could* have been is a weak pair. The founder should recognise the rejection as a real path the brand chose against.
4. **Cap at 5–7 in the brand book** even if more emerge — the artifact is a working document, not an exhaustive matrix. The discipline is which 5–7 are load-bearing for downstream design + content.
