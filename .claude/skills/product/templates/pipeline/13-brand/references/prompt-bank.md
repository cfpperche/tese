# Step 5 — Founder Interview Prompt Bank

The interview is calibrated to the founder-clarity branch detected in `prompt.md` § 1. Pull from the matching branch; don't run all branches.

## Always-ask anchors (regardless of branch)

These three earn their cost on every interview — they're the smallest set that produces an artifact:

1. **"Product name — locked, or 2–3 candidates on the table?"** → grounds the artifact's identity. If shortlist, ask which is preferred + why.
2. **"Name 1–2 brands whose voice you'd RUN AWAY FROM. Why?"** → anti-patterns are easier to articulate than the positive ideal. Always start here when the founder is stuck.
3. **"What's the single emotion the user should feel in the first 30 seconds?"** → curiosity / relief / productive / empowered / vindicated / amused — name one. If the founder names "professional", push back: that's not an emotion.

## Branch A — sharp vision (2–3 deepening probes)

The brief carries a sharp hook + named persona language + admired-brand references. The brand mostly exists — your job is to sharpen edges and surface the anti-patterns the founder hasn't articulated yet.

- **"In the brief you wrote `<exact quote>`. Walk me through the voice underneath that — what kind of person are they speaking to, and what voice would have been wrong here?"** (turns the founder's sharpest line into voice-pattern + anti-pattern in one motion)
- **"What's the one phrase a competitor uses about itself that we'd never say about ourselves?"** → anti-pattern in the founder's own market vocabulary
- **"What's a moment the brand voice would *crack* — when does the discipline cost too much?"** → tested-edge probe; surfaces nuance the brief won't show

Stop at 2–3. More on a sharp founder dilutes signal.

## Branch B — mostly-vibes (5–7 broadening probes)

The brief has a clear product-shape but voice is mostly implicit (adjectives without examples). The founder has intuition; your job is to pull voice out into concrete samples and map adjectives to anti-patterns.

- **"If this product were a person, how would they describe themselves at a dinner party? Avoid 'professional' — that means nothing. Sardonic? Earnest? Considered? Direct? What's the closest cultural reference?"**
- **"Name 2–3 existing brands whose voice you ADMIRE for similar audiences. What specifically about their voice works?"** (each admired brand is a `borrowed: <X>` data point)
- **"Same question inverted: 1–2 brands whose voice you'd RUN AWAY FROM. Why?"** (`rejected: <Y>`)
- **"What word would a satisfied user use about the product when describing it to a colleague?"** → external voice signal; usually orthogonal to the founder's internal articulation
- **"Voice anti-patterns — what should the brand NEVER sound like? Be concrete: 'never corporate jargon' is too vague. 'Never refer to users as guys' is the right level of specific."**
- **"Visual direction — strong preference (clean minimalist / playful / brutalist / corporate / hand-drawn / serif-led editorial), or open to designer interpretation?"** (visual-direction line in the brand book)
- **"Color story — preference or open? Name colors (NOT codes) plus the feeling you want."** (color-story section; deliberately no hex)

Pull 5–7 of these. If the founder rolls deeper on one, follow — depth on a single answer beats coverage of 7 shallow ones.

## Branch C — no clarity (7–9 foundational probes)

The brief is thin on identity (founder said what the product DOES but not what it FEELS like). Voice doesn't exist yet — you're co-creating it. Start from comparable brands and persona reactions.

- **"Pick 3–5 brands you instinctively respect — not necessarily competitors, any category. What do they have in common?"** (induce voice DNA from admiration patterns)
- **"Now 1–2 brands you actively dislike or distrust. What's the underlying property?"** (induce voice DNA from rejection patterns)
- **"If a user describes the product to a colleague in 10 words, what 3 of those words would make you happiest? What 3 would make you wince?"** (positive + negative voice signals via projection)
- **"Single emotion the user feels in the first 30 seconds — pick from this list and tell me why:** curiosity / relief / vindication / productivity / amusement / empowerment / mastery / belonging / control. **None of those? Name your own — but it has to be a real emotion, not 'satisfied'."**
- **"Describe a moment you've had with a digital product — could be ours, could be anyone's — where the voice made you trust it. What did it say or do?"** (concrete trust moment as voice template)
- **"Same question, inverted: a moment a product's voice made you immediately distrust or disengage. What was the trigger?"** (concrete distrust moment as anti-pattern)
- **"Are you the target user, or are you building for someone else? If someone else — describe one specific person. By name if you have one in mind."** (persona grounding; voice has to fit them, not the founder)
- **"Visual direction — pick a poster, an album cover, a website, a magazine spread that captures the feel. Anything outside the product category."** (visual direction via mood reference, not category convention)
- **"Voice anti-patterns — even if the positive voice is fuzzy, what voice would be a clear NO? Often easier to articulate."**

Pull 7–9 of these. The output is messier on a no-clarity founder — that's the nature of the input. The brand-book artifact is honest about that and may end up shorter than a sharp-vision book; that's fine.

## How to use the bank

The branches are not fixed labels. A founder may be sharp on visual direction but unclear on voice — pull the visual question from Branch A and the voice questions from Branch C. The branch is shorthand for "where do I aim the depth?", not "answer this exact set."

Always finish with:

- **"Anything you've been thinking about the brand that I haven't asked? Often the founder's own surfacing is the sharpest input we get."**
