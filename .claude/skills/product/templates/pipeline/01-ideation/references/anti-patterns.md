# Ideation Anti-Patterns

Common failures that produce weak, generic, or unfundable product concepts. Check every concept against these before presenting.

---

## 1. "Uber for X" Syndrome
**Pattern:** "[Successful company] but for [different industry]" — no original thinking, just category transplant.
**Why it fails:** The original succeeded because of specific market conditions, not because the template is magic. Investors hear 50 of these per week.
**Instead:** Identify the UNDERLYING MECHANIC that made it work, then apply it to a genuinely underserved problem. Not "Uber for dog walking" but "real-time supply-demand matching for fragmented local services with trust scoring."

## 2. Feature List Without Value Loop
**Pattern:** "It does X, Y, Z, and also has AI" — a list of features with no explanation of why someone comes back.
**Why it fails:** Features don't create retention. Value loops do.
**Instead:** Describe the EXPERIENCE: "User uploads messy data → AI cleans it in 30 seconds → user shares clean report with client → client asks 'what tool is this?' → signs up." That's a value loop.

## 3. Solution Looking for a Problem
**Pattern:** "What if we used [cool technology] to build [thing nobody asked for]?"
**Why it fails:** Technology push ≠ market pull. Nobody pays for clever engineering. They pay for solved problems.
**Instead:** Start from the pain point ("restaurant owners spend 3 hours/week managing reviews across 5 platforms") then apply technology. The pain must exist BEFORE the solution.

## 4. Ignoring Unit Economics
**Pattern:** "We'll get millions of free users and monetize later."
**Why it fails:** Free users are expensive. If CAC > LTV, growth = faster death.
**Instead:** Even in ideation, sketch: who pays, how much, and is that enough to cover acquisition cost? If the persona is a solopreneur and the product costs $10/mo, you need 10,000 paying users for $100K MRR. Is that realistic?

## 5. No Moat (Cloneable in a Weekend)
**Pattern:** "AI wrapper around [API]" or "prettier dashboard for [existing data]."
**Why it fails:** If a developer can clone the core in a weekend, there's no business — just a temporary feature advantage.
**Instead:** Every concept needs at least ONE moat: network effects, proprietary data that improves with usage, deep integrations that take months to build, or community/brand that can't be copied.

## 6. Corporate Language
**Pattern:** "Leveraging AI to optimize cross-functional productivity synergies."
**Why it fails:** Kills creative energy, sounds like every failed startup pitch deck. Nobody gets excited about "synergies."
**Instead:** Builder voice: "What if your team's Slack messages automatically became a knowledge base that answers new hires' questions?" Speak like someone excited about solving a real problem.

## 7. Boiling the Ocean (Over-Scoped)
**Pattern:** Concept requires 12 integrations, real-time multiplayer, AI, payments, and marketplace — can't validate in under 6 months.
**Why it fails:** Can't validate demand before running out of runway. The bigger the scope, the higher the risk of building something nobody wants.
**Instead:** What's the SMALLEST version that proves the core value? Can you validate with a spreadsheet + Typeform? A single API integration? A manual service before automating?

## 8. Ignoring Existing Solutions
**Pattern:** "Build a project management tool" without knowing Asana, Linear, Notion, Monday, ClickUp, Jira, and 200 others exist.
**Why it fails:** Entering a saturated market without a 10x differentiator = invisible.
**Instead:** ALWAYS search for existing solutions first. If 50 tools exist, your concept must do something genuinely novel OR serve a niche none of them target well.

## 9. Developer-Only Appeal
**Pattern:** Every concept requires a GitHub account, understanding of APIs, or programming knowledge to appreciate.
**Why it fails:** Developer market is small (30M worldwide) and hyper-competitive. B2B SMB market is 400M+ businesses.
**Instead:** At least half the concepts should target non-developers. "AI that writes proposals for HVAC contractors" has 10x the market of "AI that writes README files."

## 10. No Distribution Story
**Pattern:** "We'll build it and they will come."
**Why it fails:** Discovery is the #1 challenge for software products. A great product with no distribution plan dies in obscurity.
**Instead:** Every concept must answer: "Where do the first 100 users come from?" with a SPECIFIC answer. Not "social media" but "post in 3 specific Facebook groups for restaurant owners + cold DM 50 restaurant owners on Instagram."

## 11. Copying AI Wrappers
**Pattern:** "ChatGPT but for [domain]" — thin wrapper around an LLM with a system prompt.
**Why it fails:** OpenAI, Anthropic, Google can add this as a feature. No defensibility. Hundreds of wrappers already exist.
**Instead:** The AI must be EMBEDDED in a workflow, not the product. "AI that monitors your inventory, predicts stockouts, and auto-reorders" — the AI is invisible. The workflow is the product.

## 12. Confusing "I want this" with "The market wants this"
**Pattern:** Builder creates something they personally want, assumes others do too.
**Why it fails:** Sample size of 1. Personal itches don't always generalize.
**Instead:** Validate: search Reddit/G2 for complaints, check if competitors exist (demand signal), ask 5 potential users "would you pay $X for this?" BEFORE committing.

---

## Quick check — run every concept through this

- [ ] Can I explain it in 2 sentences to a non-technical person?
- [ ] Is there a clear Hook (try) → Retain (stay) → Refer (share)?
- [ ] Does it have at least one moat that takes months to replicate?
- [ ] Can I name 3+ people/companies who would pay for this TODAY?
- [ ] Is it NOT just an AI wrapper around an existing API?
- [ ] Can a core version be validated in < 4 weeks?
- [ ] Have I searched for existing solutions? (If 50+ exist, what's my 10x?)
- [ ] Does it work as a business at $49-99/mo? (Unit economics sketch)
- [ ] Would I use this myself? (Necessary but not sufficient)
- [ ] Do the first 100 users have a SPECIFIC acquisition path?
