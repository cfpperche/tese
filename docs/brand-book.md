# Brand Book — tese

**Status:** draft · **Date:** 2026-06-02 · 2-3 section snapshot (personal-tool scale)

## Name

**tese** (working name) — Portuguese for *thesis*. You form an investment **thesis** and the tool
helps you watch it, hold it, and keep the record straight. Short, lowercase, calm. Alternatives
considered: `norte` (compass/north), `alfa` (alpha). `tese` wins for capturing the *decision-support*
posture — it's about *your* reasoning, not the tool's opinion.

## Positioning (one line)

*A calm, private ledger that keeps you decision-ready and filing-ready — never telling you what to
buy.*

## Voice & tone

- **Calm, precise, never hyped.** This is a financial instrument for a capable owner — no rocket
  emojis, no "to the moon," no urgency manufacturing. The BTC chapter is exactly the noise this
  tool is the antidote to.
- **Plain and exact.** Numbers with provenance; states labeled ("data as of…", "your recorded FX
  rate"). When uncertain, say so ("confirm with your contador").
- **Second person, quiet authority.** "You hold", "you recorded", "you crossed US$ 60k" — the user
  is the decider; the tool reports.

**Voice samples**
- Empty state: *"No holdings yet. Log your first remittance to start the record."*
- Threshold flag: *"Your US-situs assets just crossed US$ 60k. Worth reading up on estate-tax
  exposure and UCITS alternatives — not urgent, but on the radar now."*
- Export done: *"Your 2026 record is ready: 4 positions, 3 remittances, 2 dividends. Hand it to
  your contador."*

## We are / we are not

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Decision-**support** — surfaces data | An advisor — no signals, scores, or picks |
| A Brazil-aware compliance **ledger** | A tax filer — the contador files |
| Calm, private, local-first | Cloud, social, gamified |
| Honest about uncertainty | A source of "guaranteed" anything |
| Read-only, always | A trading terminal |

## Visual direction (posture)

- **Mood:** quiet financial instrument — think a well-set table of numbers, not a trading HUD.
  Dark-neutral or warm-paper background, restrained accents, generous whitespace, monospace for
  figures so columns align and numbers read precisely.
- **Color discipline:** one calm brand accent + semantic green/red used **only** for P&L and
  day-change (so the only "loud" color carries real meaning). Flags (thresholds) get a distinct
  amber — informational, not alarming.
- **Typography:** a clean humanist sans for UI text; a monospace for all monetary/numeric values.
- **No chart junk:** sparse axes, no gradients-for-decoration; the data is the design.
